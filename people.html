About Dr. Soni

Dr. Purushottam Soni leads the Soni Microfluidics & Interfacial Engineering Laboratory (SMILE Lab) in the E. S. Witchger School of Engineering at Marian University. His research focuses on developing innovative engineering technologies to investigate complex physical and biological systems through microfluidic engineering, electrohydrodynamics, interfacial science, and advanced materials. Current research in the laboratory includes electrohydrodynamics of complex fluids, microfluidic technologies for environmental toxicology and transgenerational health, and flexible polymer nanocomposite thin films.

Prior to joining Marian University, Dr. Soni served as a Visiting Assistant Professor of Chemical Engineering at Rose-Hulman Institute of Technology and completed postdoctoral research at Texas Tech University, where he conducted interdisciplinary research in microfluidics, environmental toxicology, healthy aging, and space biology. He earned his Ph.D. in Chemical Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology Bombay in 2018.

Committed to undergraduate education, Dr. Soni believes that meaningful engineering education extends beyond the classroom. Through hands-on research experiences, he mentors undergraduate students in engineering design, experimentation, quantitative analysis, and scientific communication while fostering curiosity, creativity, and interdisciplinary collaboration.
